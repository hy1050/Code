Theoretical foundations of Model

A split-flap display, often known as a flap display, is an outstanding installation: the combination of electromechanical, artistic, and technological elements. It frequently used as a public transportation schedule at airports and train stations in Central European countries. The familiar 'clack clack clack' sound, the gentle freeze from rotating flap cards and character information displays would all amaze onlookers.

What's new ?

Each split-flap display is carefully crafted for your unique project. Every module contains 64 alphanumeric flaps in Vietnamese with accent marks and the English alphabet, colors, and some punctuation marks. The basic displays with 6 letters, each unit has 16cm Height x 9.5cm Width. You can place the displays on shelf or into frames.
